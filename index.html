Choose your allignment

The alignments are usually two words long. Let’s take the example of Lawful Good.
The first part of the alignment, ‘Lawful’ describes that alignment’s ethics. The ethics section of the alignment indicates that character’s perspective on society as a whole. Do they respect authority or rebel against it? Do they like order and hierarchy or prefer individuality and freedom?
The second part of the alignment, ‘Good’, describes that alignment’s morals. Will they trample on others to get what they want, or do they want to help others? Will they hurt people for fun or will they protect them at all costs?

Lawful Good

Lawful Good characters always do the right thing as expected by society. They always follow the rules, tell the truth and help people out. They like order, trust and believe in people with social authority, and they aim to be an upstanding citizen.

Lawful Neutral

The Lawful Neutral character does not care about what others think of their actions, they only care about their actions being correct according to their code. But they do not preach their code to others and try to convert them.

Lawful Evil

Lawful Evil characters operate within a strict code of laws and traditions. Upholding these values and living by these is more important than anything, even the lives of others. They may not consider themselves to be Evil, they may believe what they are doing is right.

Neutral Good

While they will generally follow the rules and laws of society, they do so because they lead to good outcomes, rather than because they feel obligated to by authority figures and institutions. A Neutral Good person will break the rules if they are doing it for good reasons and they will feel confident and justified in their actions.

True Neutral

True Neutral (sometimes called Neutral Neutral) characters don’t like to take sides. They are pragmatic rather than emotional in their actions, choosing the response which makes the most sense for them in each situation.

Neutral Evil

Neutral Evil characters are selfish. Their actions are driven by their own wants whether that’s power, greed, attention, or something else. They will follow laws if they happen to align with their ambitions, but they will not hesitate to break them if they don’t.

Chaotic Good

Chaotic Good characters do what their conscience tells them to for the greater good. They do not care about following society’s rules, they care about doing what’s right.

Chaotic Neutral

Chaotic Neutral characters do what is most likely to ensure their freedom, protect their free will, and get them the outcome they want. That might be a Good action like helping someone, or an Evil action like failing to tell the truth.

Chaotic Evil

Chaotic Evil characters care only for themselves with a complete disregard for all law and order and for the welfare and freedom of others. They harm others out of anger or just for fun.

Terrain

Terrain or relief involves the vertical and horizontal dimensions of land surface. The term bathymetry is used to describe underwater relief, while hypsometry studies terrain relative to sea level. The Latin word terra means "earth."

Flame Desert

This thornbush savannah covers 2,485 miles, and the grassy plains are covered in thick grass with very few trees. The dry season lasts for 8 months and the wet season brings just enough rain to replenish the land. It is home to many common species of animals, including many different small animals living in herds of over 8,000 individuals.

The Greens

This small farmland is over 50 years old and was once home to an old settlement. The soil is extremely fertile, making it suitable for growing excessive amounts of crops. It is carefully managed and is home to a wide range of different plants and species of animals. In the midst of the farmland is a long abandoned village.

Woods of Ita

This moderate humid, sparse forest is around 18 miles from North to South, and roughly 105 miles across from East to West, covering almost 1,890 sq miles. The water between the trees is covered with algae, fruit trees and edible plants are few and far between, and safe drinking water is not difficult to find.

Mangira

This large desert is around 1,069 miles from North to South, and roughly 594 miles across from East to West, covering almost 634,986 sq miles. It has many volcanoes that take the temperature to scorching degrees. It is home to hardly any life

Icehills

This sprawling Alpine tundra is around 339 miles from North to South, and roughly 359 miles across from East to West, covering almost 121,701 sq miles. It is home to around 1000 hardy plants and 100 species of animals, including 6 large species. This tundra is notable for its abundant flowers. Deep within the tundra, rare metals can be found.

BLUEWild

This enormous, misty jungle is home to an unknown amount of different plants and animals; barely any of these are venomous or poisonous. 15 species of large predators make their home here. The tallest trees are around 227 feet / 69 metres tall, and the overgrowth is very difficult to travel through without cutting tools.

Peaks of Kor

This mountain range is 4,623 miles long, reaches 22,254 feet at its highest point, and there are many sheer cliffs and drops. There are no known paths between the mountains. They are all made of pure gold. Their peaks reflect sunlight creating many suns in the sky.

Atlantis

The kingdom of Atlantis is around 11,656 miles from North to South, and roughly 4,091 miles across from East to West, covering almost 47,684,696 sq miles. The water is deep, with an average depth of 43,483 feet. It is home to different marine plants and species of animals.
